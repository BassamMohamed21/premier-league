Arsenal History

Arsenal Football Club is a professional football club based in North London, England. The club was founded in 1886 by workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, and it moved to its current home at the Emirates Stadium in 2006. Throughout its history, Arsenal has been one of the most successful clubs in English football, with a long list of domestic and international honours. Here are some of the key moments in the club's history: - In 1930-31, Arsenal became the first club to win the league championship with a record-breaking 66 points. - In the 1930s, Arsenal won five league championships and two FA Cups, establishing the club as a major force in English football. - In 1971, Arsenal won the league and cup double for the first time in the club's history, a feat that was repeated in 1998 and 2002. - In 1989, Arsenal won the league championship with a last-minute goal against Liverpool at Anfield, in a match that has become known as the "Anfield '89" game. - In 1991, Arsenal won the European Cup Winners' Cup, beating Parma 1-0 in the final. - In 2003-04, Arsenal won the league championship without losing a single game, a feat that had not been achieved in English football since the 19th century. This team became known as "The Invincibles". - In recent years, Arsenal has continued to compete at the highest level of English football, although the club has not won the league championship since 2004. Overall, Arsenal has won 13 league championships, 14 FA Cups, 2 League Cups, and 1 European Cup Winners' Cup. The club also holds the record for the longest unbeaten run in English football, with 49 games without defeat between 2003 and 2004. Arsenal has a rich history and a large and passionate fanbase, both in England and around the world.
