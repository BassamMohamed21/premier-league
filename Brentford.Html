Brentford History

Brentford Football Club is a professional football club based in Brentford, West London, England. The club was founded in 1889 and has played its home games at the Brentford Community Stadium since 2020. Throughout its history, Brentford has been a relatively small club compared to some of its more illustrious London rivals, but it has a proud tradition and a loyal fanbase. Here are some of the key moments in the club's history: - In 1929-30, Brentford reached the FA Cup quarter-finals for the first time in the club's history, but lost to Southampton in a replay. - In 1934-35, Brentford finished in fifth place in the top division of English football, which remains the club's highest league finish to date. - In 1941, Brentford won the Football League War Cup, beating Portsmouth 3-2 in the final. - In the 1960s and 1970s, Brentford suffered a decline and spent much of the time in the lower divisions of English football. - In the 1990s, Brentford began to rebuild under the ownership of Ron Noades and later Matthew Benham. The club moved to a new stadium, Griffin Park, in 1904, which became a fortress for the team. - In recent years, Brentford has been on an upward trajectory. The club reached the Championship playoffs in 2020, narrowly missing out on promotion to the Premier League. However, they gained promotion in 2021, after winning the Championship playoff final. Overall, Brentford has never won a major trophy in English football, but the club has a proud history and a loyal fanbase. Brentford has a reputation for playing attractive and attacking football, and the club has a bright future under the ownership of Matthew Benham.
